3) Investment Analysis — Chain of Thought

a) Concerns (what could break the case)

  • Seasonality; risk 10% S&M insufficient to maintain lead flow.
  • Device downtime >10% for 30+ days.
  • Key-provider dependency: injector churn >15% in first 90 days.

b) Opportunities

  • Memberships & pricing governance to lift LTV and revenue/hour.
  • Injector productivity ladder (utilization, mix, scheduling discipline).

c) Adjustments

  • S&M to 10% rev; owner +$300; remove one-offs; reclass interest; add back depreciation → $688.

d) Fair multiple

  • 7.0× (band 6–8×) — cash-pay, ~70% GP, recurring mix vs single-site risk.

e) Other methods

  • EPV, simple DCF, comps/precedents bracket 6–8×; support 7× midpoint.

f) Cash vs Equity split

  • 70/30 improves retention/alignment; preserves growth capex and leverage headroom.

g) Owner compensation impact

  • Keep market post-close comp; add back only the excess ($300). Producer continuity secured via employment + rollover.

6) Due Diligence Priorities (30–45 Days)

  • QoE & revenue recognition — validate $688; seasonality; one-offs.
  • Debt & debt-like — loan/lease schedules; payoff letters; gift cards/prepaids; fees/taxes; cash at close.
  • Working capital — NWC peg = LTM monthly average; purchase price adjustment.
  • Provider agreements — employment terms, non-competes, schedules; rollover intentions.
  • Device fleet — uptime logs; SLAs; near-term capex.
  • Marketing analytics — lead sources; CPL/CPA; cohort retention; membership penetration.

Each item closes a direct valuation risk: QoE → EBITDA; debt-like/NWC → equity; provider/device → run-rate stability.
